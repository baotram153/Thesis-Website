## Abstract

[End-to-end autonomous driving](https://github.com/opendrivelab/end-to-end-autonomous-driving) policies have demonstrated strong performance by directly mapping raw sensor observations to low-level control commands. However, many state-of-the-art models rely on large network architectures and extensive computational resources, limiting their applicability in real-time and embedded systems. This thesis focuses on the development of a **lightweight end-to-end driving policy** that achieves **competitive performance** while significantly reducing model complexity.

We propose a training framework that combines [knowledge distillation](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2006.05525) and [reinforcement learning](https://web.stanford.edu/class/psych209/Readings/SuttonBartoIPRLBook2ndEd.pdf) fine-tuning to bridge the performance gap between high-capacity teacher models and efficient student policies. In the distillation stage, a compact student network is trained to imitate the behavior of a stronger teacher. This process provides a stable baseline that mitigates optimization difficulty and reduces sample inefficiency during subsequent learning. After that, we further refine the student model through reinforcement learning fine-tuning. By allowing direct interaction with the environment, reinforcement learning enables the policy to adapt beyond imitation, improving [robustness and causal reasoning](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2306.16927#subsection.4.8) while preserving the efficiency of the lightweight architecture. This combination allows the student to retain the teacher’s structured behavior while autonomously optimizing performance under closed-loop feedback.

All methods are evaluated in a [closed-loop simulation environment](https://github.com/Thinklab-SJTU/Bench2Drive#:~:text=%5B2025/02/05,planning%20as%20well.), where the agent’s actions directly influence future states, reflecting the dynamic nature of real-world driving. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed approach effectively enhances the performance of lightweight policies.
</HighlightedSection>
